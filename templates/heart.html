High Risk

Cardiovascular disease is the leading global cause of death, accounting for 17.3 million deaths per year.

High Cost

Direct and indirect costs of cardiovascular diseases and stroke total more than $320.1 billion.

Less Exercise

In fact, lack of exercise is the single worst risk factor for heart disease for both men and women. Despite this, 70 per cent of adults don't do enough exercise.

Younger Age

About 20 percent, were 40 or younger. In addition, patients 40 and younger were just as likely to die after their heart attack as those ages 41 to 50.

Easy to Ignore
